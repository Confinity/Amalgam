# Conversational Copy Guide (ICP-Focused)

## Who We Are Writing For
- Founder-led teams where shipping slowed after growth, a raise, or stack complexity.
- Product and engineering leaders carrying delivery risk across teams.
- Enterprise modernization teams dealing with fragile systems and execution pressure.

## Voice Rules
- Write like a senior operator in a real working session, not a marketing brochure.
- Ask direct questions in headers when it helps users self-identify quickly.
- Name concrete problems: releases slipping, brittle architecture, integration drag, unclear ownership.
- Prefer plain verbs: fix, ship, decide, unblock, sequence.
- Keep each section anchored to one user outcome and one next action.

## Copy Patterns That Work
- Hero question: "Are releases slipping even though your team is busy?"
- Problem framing: "Here is what is likely causing the slowdown."
- Decision framing: "What should you do first?"
- CTA framing: "Start with a strategy call" or "Run the 5-minute diagnostic"

## Phrases To Avoid
- Vague abstractions without context: "clarity", "momentum", "transformation" by themselves.
- Overused consulting language: "unlock", "best-in-class", "synergy", "leverage" unless truly necessary.
- Generic promises that hide scope: "we optimize outcomes" without naming what changes.

## Site-Wide Consistency Rules
- Use "product delivery" or "releases" when referring to execution speed.
- Use "system complexity" when discussing architecture/integration burden.
- Use "next move" for decision-oriented CTAs.
- Keep CTA labels consistent across routes.
- Keep Launchpad language practical: stage -> pressure -> next action.

## Launchpad UX Writing Rules
- Every module should answer one question: "What should I do next?"
- Each diagnostic result must include:
  - What this profile means in plain language
  - Why it matters now
  - First 2-week actions
  - Clear next path (self-serve or strategy call)
- Keep pressure filters explicit and optional.

## Ongoing QA Checklist
- Can a founder understand the page in 10 seconds?
- Is each headline specific to a real operating problem?
- Is every section tied to one concrete outcome?
- Is there one clear CTA and one secondary CTA only?
- Does the copy still sound human when read out loud?
