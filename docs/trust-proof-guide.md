# Trust and Proof Guide

## Current Classification
## Highly Persuasive
- `CaseStudiesExplorer` filtering and relevance model (`components/sections/CaseStudiesExplorer.tsx`).
- Stage-specific proof inside `Your Next Move` workspace (`components/launchpad/ActiveStageWorkspace.tsx`).
- Contact response-time reassurance (`components/contact/ContactMethodsStrip.tsx`).

## Moderately Persuasive
- Testimonial cards (`lib/testimonials.ts`) with named roles and companies.
- Logo credibility bands (`components/ui/TrustBand.tsx`).
- Offer progression timeline (`components/sections/EngagementTimeline.tsx`).

## Decorative or Underleveraged
- Repeated trust band usage with very similar framing on multiple pages.
- Offer detail pages with low concrete proof density.
- Some case-study cards with narrative outcomes but limited measurable evidence.

## Repetitive
- Repeated trust claim headline `Trusted when execution gets hard`.
- Repeated generic escalation statement `Need a clear next step?`.

## Keep
- Situation-first case-study architecture.
- Stage-aware proof references in `Your Next Move`.
- Human response-time promise on contact route.

## Tighten
- Replace broad trust claims with concrete proof atoms:
  - context,
  - intervention,
  - measurable change.
- Require at least one quantifiable metric per featured case-study card.

## Move
- Move proof closer to decision points:
  - One proof tile directly above each offer CTA.
  - One proof tile directly above final CTA on key pages.

## Reduce
- Reduce repeated full trust-band blocks across top pages.
- Keep one full trust-band on homepage, then use compact proof snippets elsewhere.

## Make More Specific
- Replace generic testimonial intros (`Client perspective`) with problem-specific labels.
- Add proof tags:
  - `Reduced lead time`,
  - `Improved reporting trust`,
  - `Resolved integration drag`.

## Trust Placement Rules
- Before asking for escalation, show one relevant proof object.
- Do not place two large trust sections back-to-back.
- Use one dominant proof narrative per page, not multiple equal-weight proof clusters.
- Align proof type to page intent:
  - `Services` -> offer-fit outcomes.
  - `Our Work` -> situation match + measurable deltas.
  - `Contact` -> process trust and response reliability.
