# Amalgam Copy Standards

## Voice Target
Sound like:
- A calm operator.
- A sharp strategic partner.
- A serious, human team.
- People who diagnose real blockers and recommend practical moves.

Do not sound like:
- A generic consultancy selling frameworks.
- AI-expanded filler prose.
- Corporate abstraction without operational specifics.

## Headline Rules
- One idea per headline.
- Target 6 to 12 words.
- Prefer concrete pressure and concrete move.
- Avoid stacked abstractions (`clarity`, `outcomes`, `complexity`) unless grounded by specifics.
- Use sentence case unless brand style requires otherwise.

## Support-Line Rules
- One sentence by default.
- Target 14 to 24 words.
- Explain what changes for the user, not what the company believes.
- Remove secondary qualifier clauses unless they change the decision.

## CTA Rules
- Primary CTA must state action + expected outcome.
- Secondary CTA must be materially different, not a synonym.
- Avoid repeating the same CTA label across unrelated contexts.
- Include context in CTA labels where possible (`Book founder review call`, `Run delivery diagnostic`).

## Anti-AI Copy Checks (Pass/Fail)
- Does this block repeat the same idea in two sentences?
- Are there broad claims without an operational noun (`map`, `decision`, `handoff`, `metric`, `owner`)?
- Could one sentence be deleted with no loss of meaning?
- Are there stacked vague verbs (`unlock`, `navigate`, `drive`) without specific objects?
- Is the emotional tone authored and direct, or merely plausible?

## Rewrite Pattern
1. Keep only one claim per block.
2. Name the operational object.
3. Name the decision or next move.
4. Remove hedge words.
5. Compress to shortest complete form.

## Current Copy Tightening Examples
## Example 1
- Before: `Need a clear next step?`
- After: `Get a direct recommendation for your next move.`
- Why: clearer user outcome than generic `clear`.

## Example 2
- Before: `Use Your Next Move to self-serve, or book a strategy call for a direct recommendation.`
- After: `Map your stage in minutes, or bring your context for a direct recommendation.`
- Why: tighter rhythm and clearer contrast of paths.

## Example 3
- Before: `Teams use Amalgam to restore delivery confidence, stabilize systems, and keep momentum moving.`
- After: `Teams bring us in to fix delivery drag, stabilize fragile systems, and restore decision confidence.`
- Why: stronger operational verbs.

## Example 4
- Before: `We identify the blocker, recommend the right next step, and go deeper only where support is needed.`
- After: `We find the blocker, recommend the next move, and only add depth where it changes results.`
- Why: removes passive language.

## Example 5
- Before: `Book a strategy call and we will recommend the most useful next step.`
- After: `Book a strategy call and leave with one clear next move.`
- Why: explicit outcome.

## Example 6
- Before: `Execution pressure is rising. Start with the guide path that matches your situation.`
- After: `When delivery pressure rises, start with the guide for your exact situation.`
- Why: natural rhythm, less formal.

## Example 7
- Before: `If self-serve is not enough, choose the support level that fits right now.`
- After: `If diagnostics are not enough, choose the support level that fits this stage.`
- Why: more concrete decision frame.

## Example 8
- Before: `Start with this briefing`
- After: `Read this briefing first`
- Why: simpler, more natural CTA.

## Example 9
- Before: `Talk through a situation like this`
- After: `Discuss a similar case`
- Why: shorter, less repetitive.

## Example 10
- Before: `No drawn-out sales process`
- After: `No long sales cycle`
- Why: tighter and direct.

## Enforcement Standard
- Every page opening gets one headline + one support sentence + two CTA max.
- Every CTA label must be unique enough to infer user intent in analytics.
- Every final CTA band must avoid generic `Need a clear next step?` phrasing.
