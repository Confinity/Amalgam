What was the core constraint in the FINRA engagement?+

What moved forward after implementation?+

Which implementation choices mattered most in this engagement?+

The work centered on Enterprise Systems and Regulatory Technology. The choices mattered because they supported the real operating constraint instead of adding unnecessary complexity.

How do you keep this kind of work practical instead of over-engineered?+

By sequencing around the highest-leverage constraint, using technology choices that fit the operating context, and staying focused on what actually needs to move for the team.

How do you handle legacy modernization pressure safely?+

By treating modernization as a sequenced operating change, not a single technical event. Work starts with the most critical risk and expands once continuity is protected.

What helps teams avoid disruption during platform transitions?+

Clear migration sequencing, explicit ownership, and practical change-management support. This engagement reflected that with emphasis on "Technology Assessment".

How do you improve reliability in a live operating environment?+

By tightening core system boundaries, improving workflow consistency, and making day-to-day operations easier to run with confidence.

Why did "Technology Assessment" matter in this engagement?+

Evaluated key technical infrastructure and identified practical improvement opportunities tied to regulatory operations. That mattered because it directly addressed constraints such as: Operating at scale with numerous broker-dealers and representatives.
